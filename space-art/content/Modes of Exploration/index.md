The images from the missions that humans have launched and have explored the universe are striking, but this exploration would have been impossible without the incredible machines that have enabled this exploration. There is something beautiful about the purpose of these machines- every spacecraft being built for its specific purpose - whether that be carrying humans to destinations closer to Earth or to travel to the edges of the solar system autonomously. The machines that we send to explore the cosmos are purpose built - simply designed to complete the mission that they are assigned with the most reliable solution possible. The focus is not necessarily on aesthetics, but most spacecraft have a kind of unique utilitarian beauty to the design of these vehicles. 

Although these vehicles are designed by engineers, the unique design that is required to adapt to the harsh environment of space is unlike anything else and has a beauty of its own. By looking at specific spacecraft, one can often tell what purpose that they serve. By looking at the Space Shuttle’s design, for example, you can tell that its purpose is to carry large payloads into Low Earth orbit. With other spacecraft, such as the Voyager space probes or the Mars rovers, the design of the vehicles implies motion- both physically- as the rovers have wheels- but also in their layouts- these types of vehicles being intentionally designed by their creators to be reliable and long-lasting, allowing us to learn as much as possible for as long as possible.
