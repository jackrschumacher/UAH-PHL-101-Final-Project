When both astronauts have looked back at the Earth from Space, they are often struck by just how small our planet appears against the vast blackness of the universe that surrounds it. Our home planet appears as an oasis in the vast blackness of space- a fragile marble that contrasts against the deep blackness. This has always been something those who have left our planet, and particularly those who have traveled beyond the immediacy of Low Earth Orbit. It is not only astronauts that have changed our view of the planet that we all call home- as interplanetary probes have traveled much further and for a longer duration of time than any human mission has ever or will travel for a long time. The further that these unmanned missions travel from Earth, the smaller our planet appears and the smaller that it appears in the context of the universe as a whole. The Earth in particular is a unique planet in both our Solar System as well as other similar systems located near ours-  Earth is one of the only planets that can support life for a sustained period of time. 

From this perspective- the fragile Earth suspended in the blackness of space- being the only planet that can truly support life making it unique and special in the grand scheme. It becomes clear just how special that the world that we live in is compared in the grand emptiness that surrounds it.
