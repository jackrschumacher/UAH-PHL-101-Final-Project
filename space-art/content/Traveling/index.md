The Universe is vast, and with current technology, it is unrealistic for humans to venture much further beyond planetary bodies that are within a few years of Earth. In order to explore beyond, we rely on probes- automated machines that we have built in order to accomplish a specific mission - sometimes even one that evolves as the mission continues. Even though the images, or sometimes visualizations that we create back on earth from other instruments - such as radar mapping and more. This data can then be used to generate visualizations that can show us what a planet may look like- even when there is no way that a camera could capture the detail in the same way. 

These probes change societies worldview- allowing society to see planets at the edge of the Solar System - planets that for thousands of years we have only seen with telescopes or as a small flicker of light in the night sky- planets that probes can now return data on to Earth. These probes represent a shift in how humans explore- for all of human history we ourselves have done the exploring- crossed the oceans, climbed the mountain, and flew the Atlantic. Although the data returned is not returned by human hands- the probes that return the data were built by humans and thus generate art. They show us unique worlds- no two that we have found appear the same.
